Fermi Bubbles & Sagittarius A*: Shock-Driven Modelling

Overview

Built analytic + numerical models of supernova remnant (SNR) phases (free expansion, Sedov–Taylor, snow-plow) and applied them to the Galaxy’s Fermi Bubbles to study expansion behaviour and energy injection from SMBH feedback.

Approach

  • Derived phase-wise evolution (R, v, E) for SNRs and mapped parameters to bubble scales.
  • Explored density dependence and self-similar behaviour for radius vs time.
  • Discussed role of Sgr A* activity and gas accretion in sustaining energetics.

Key Findings

  • Constant-velocity early phase → adiabatic deceleration; parameters tuned to reproduce bubble-scale structure.
  • Energy growth scenarios consistent with continued SMBH accretion/feedback.
