Quantum Mechanics: A Gentle Start

A comprehensive, intuition‑first set of notes: foundations and postulates, Schrödinger equation, boundary conditions, 1D model systems (free particle, step, barrier, finite/infinite wells), tunnelling, uncertainty, operators and commutators, Fourier/momentum space, harmonic oscillator, 2D/3D wells and degeneracy, angular momentum, spherical harmonics, and radial probability.

Postulates & State Space

  • States: A (pure) state is a vector \(|\psi\rangle\) in a complex Hilbert space. Physical predictions depend on the ray (overall phase is unobservable).
  • Observables: Represented by Hermitian operators \(\hat O\). Measurement outcomes are eigenvalues \(o\) with probabilities given by the Born rule.
  • Born rule: In position space, probability density is \(|\psi(x,t)|^2\). Expectation \(\langle O\rangle=\langle\psi|\hat O|\psi\rangle\).
  • Dynamics: Time evolution is unitary: \(|\psi(t)\rangle=\hat U(t,t_0)|\psi(t_0)\rangle\), with \(\hat U=e^{-\tfrac{i}{\hbar}\hat H (t-t_0)}\).

Operators, Hermiticity & Commutators

  • Position \(\hat x\) and momentum \(\hat p=-i\hbar\,\partial_x\) obey \([\hat x,\hat p]=i\hbar\).
  • Hermitian (self‑adjoint) \(\hat O\) has real eigenvalues and orthonormal eigenkets; completeness allows expansions \(|\psi\rangle=\sum_n c_n |n\rangle\).
  • Generalised eigenstates (e.g., momentum) are \(\delta\)-normalised: \(\langle p'|p\rangle=\delta(p-p')\).
  • Robertson inequality gives for any \(\hat A,\hat B\): \(\Delta A\,\Delta B\ge\tfrac12|\langle[\hat A,\hat B]\rangle|\).

Schrödinger Equation

\[ i\hbar\,\frac{\partial}{\partial t}\,\psi(x,t)=\left(-\frac{\hbar^2}{2m}\,\frac{\partial^2}{\partial x^2}+V(x,t)\right)\psi(x,t). \]

For time‑independent \(V(x)\), separate variables \(\psi(x,t)=u(x)\,e^{-iEt/\hbar}\) to get the time‑independent equation

\[ -\frac{\hbar^2}{2m}\,\frac{d^2u}{dx^2}+V(x)u=E\,u. \]

Boundary & Continuity Conditions

  • \(u\) must be single‑valued, finite, square‑integrable (for bound states).
  • For finite potentials, \(u\) and \(u'\) are continuous; at a finite jump in \(V\), \(u\) is continuous while \(u'\) adjusts to enforce the equation.
  • At an infinite wall, \(u=0\) at the boundary.

Free Particle & Momentum Space

For \(V=0\), plane‑wave solutions \(\psi\propto e^{i(kx-\omega t)}\) with \(E=\tfrac{\hbar^2k^2}{2m}\). The Fourier transform connects \(\psi(x)\) and momentum‑space \(\phi(p)\).

\[ \phi(p)=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi\hbar}}\int\! e^{-ipx/\hbar}\,\psi(x)\,dx,\qquad \psi(x)=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi\hbar}}\int\! e^{ipx/\hbar}\,\phi(p)\,dp. \]

Potential Step

For a step \(V(x)=0\) (\(x<0\)), \(V_0\) (\(x\ge0\)), match at \(x=0\). For \(E>V_0\) the reflection/transmission coefficients are

\[ R=\left(\frac{k_1-k_2}{k_1+k_2}\right)^2,\qquad T=\frac{4k_1k_2}{(k_1+k_2)^2},\quad k_{1,2}=\sqrt{\tfrac{2m}{\hbar^2}(E,\ E-V_0)}. \]

For \(E<V_0\), solutions decay in \(x>0\) and total reflection occurs (no probability current through \(x=0\)).

Square Barrier & Tunnelling

For a barrier of height \(V_0\) and width \(a\), with \(E<V_0\), the transmission coefficient is

\[ T=\frac{1}{1+\frac{V_0^2\sinh^2(\kappa a)}{4E(V_0-E)}},\qquad \kappa=\sqrt{\tfrac{2m}{\hbar^2}(V_0-E)}. \]

In the opaque‑barrier limit, \(T\approx 16\,\frac{E}{V_0}\left(1-\frac{E}{V_0}\right)\,e^{-2\kappa a}\). Continuity at each interface determines coefficients; probability current is conserved, \(R+T=1\).

Finite & Infinite Square Wells

Infinite well \([0,a]\)

\[ u_n(x)=\sqrt{\frac{2}{a}}\sin\!\left(\frac{n\pi x}{a}\right),\quad E_n=\frac{n^2\pi^2\hbar^2}{2ma^2},\quad n=1,2,3,\dots \]

Finite symmetric well

Inside: \(k=\sqrt{\tfrac{2m}{\hbar^2}(E+|V_0|)}\), outside: decay constant \(\kappa=\sqrt{\tfrac{2m}{\hbar^2}|E|}\). Even/odd solutions obey transcendental equations

\[ k\tan(ka)=\kappa\quad (\text{even}),\qquad -k\cot(ka)=\kappa\quad (\text{odd}). \]

Bound states exist only for a discrete set of \(E\). As \(|V_0|\) or \(a\) increase, more bound levels appear.

Uncertainty Relations

From \([\hat x,\hat p]=i\hbar\), the position–momentum uncertainty is

\[ \Delta x\,\Delta p\ge\frac{\hbar}{2}. \]

More generally, \(\Delta A\,\Delta B\ge\tfrac12|\langle[\hat A,\hat B]\rangle|\). Minimising Gaussians saturate the \(x\)–\(p\) bound.

Harmonic Oscillator

With \(V=\tfrac12 m\omega^2 x^2\), define ladder operators \(\hat a=\tfrac{1}{\sqrt{2\hbar m\omega}}(m\omega\hat x+i\hat p)\), \(\hat a^\dagger\). Then

\[ \hat H=\hbar\omega\left(\hat a^\dagger\hat a+\tfrac12\right),\qquad E_n=\hbar\omega\left(n+\tfrac12\right),\ n=0,1,2,\dots \]

Eigenfunctions involve Hermite polynomials; coherent states are displaced Gaussians with classical‑like motion.

2D/3D Wells & Degeneracy

For separable boxes \(V(x,y,z)=V_x(x)+V_y(y)+V_z(z)\), energies add:

\[ E_{n_x n_y n_z}=\frac{\hbar^2\pi^2}{2m}\left(\frac{n_x^2}{a^2}+\frac{n_y^2}{b^2}+\frac{n_z^2}{c^2}\right). \]

Symmetry (e.g., \(a=b\)) yields degeneracies such as \((n_x,n_y)=(1,2)\) and \((2,1)\).

Spherical Coordinates & Angular Momentum

In 3D, separate \(\psi(r,\theta,\phi)=R(r)Y(\theta,\phi)\). Angular momentum operators satisfy

\[ [\hat L_i,\hat L_j]=i\hbar\,\varepsilon_{ijk}\,\hat L_k,\qquad \hat{\mathbf L}^2 Y_{\ell m}=\hbar^2\ell(\ell+1)Y_{\ell m},\quad \hat L_z Y_{\ell m}=\hbar m Y_{\ell m}. \]

Spherical Harmonics

Orthornormal functions on the sphere labelled by integers \(\ell\ge0\) and \(m=-\ell,\dots,\ell\). They form the angular part of central‑potential eigenfunctions and encode rotational symmetry.

Radial Equation & Probability

The radial equation (for a central \(V(r)\)) includes the effective potential \(\tfrac{\hbar^2\ell(\ell+1)}{2mr^2}\). With normalised \(R(r)\), the radial probability density is

\[ P(r)=r^2 |R(r)|^2,\qquad \int_0^\infty P(r)\,dr=1. \]

Expectations follow from \(\langle r^n\rangle=\int_0^\infty r^n P(r)\,dr\).

Practice Questions

  • Show that Gaussian wave packets minimise \(\Delta x\,\Delta p\). Compute \(\langle x\rangle\), \(\langle p\rangle\), and widths.
  • For the step potential with \(E>V_0\), derive \(R\) and \(T\) by matching boundary conditions.
  • Compute bound‑state energies of a finite well numerically from the even/odd transcendental equations.
  • Use ladder operators to find \(\langle x^2\rangle\) in \(|n\rangle\) of the oscillator.
  • Prove orthonormality of \(Y_{\ell m}\) and evaluate \(\int Y_{\ell m}^*\,\cos\theta\,Y_{\ell' m'}\,d\Omega\).
