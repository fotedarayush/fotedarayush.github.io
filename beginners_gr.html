Basics (One‑Page Overview)

General Relativity (GR) says gravity is not a force but spacetime geometry. A metric \(g_{\mu\nu}\) measures intervals ("distances") via \(\mathrm d s^2 = g_{\mu\nu}\,\mathrm d x^{\mu}\,\mathrm d x^{\\nu}\). Free particles follow geodesics (the straightest possible worldlines), determined by the metric’s connection \(\Gamma^{\rho}{}_{\mu\nu}\). Spacetime curvature—encoded by the Riemann tensor—is sourced by energy and momentum according to Einstein’s field equations \[ G_{\mu\nu} + \Lambda g_{\mu\nu} = \frac{8\pi G}{c^4}\,T_{\mu\nu}. \] In the weak‑field, slow‑motion limit this reproduces Newton’s gravity; in strong fields it predicts black holes, gravitational waves, and an expanding Universe.
