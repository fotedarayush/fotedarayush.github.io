Beginner’s Guide to General Relativity

A streamlined, two‑layer introduction: first the basics (intuition + minimal equations), then four mathematical parts you can expand inline. Equations are typeset with MathJax.

Basics (One‑Page Overview)

General Relativity (GR) says gravity is not a force but spacetime geometry. A metric \(g_{\mu\nu}\) measures intervals ("distances") via \(\mathrm d s^2 = g_{\mu\nu}\,\mathrm d x^{\mu}\,\mathrm d x^{\\nu}\). Free particles follow geodesics (the straightest possible worldlines), determined by the metric’s connection \(\Gamma^{\rho}{}_{\mu\nu}\). Spacetime curvature—encoded by the Riemann tensor—is sourced by energy and momentum according to Einstein’s field equations \[ G_{\mu\nu} + \Lambda g_{\mu\nu} = \frac{8\pi G}{c^4}\,T_{\mu\nu}. \] In the weak‑field, slow‑motion limit this reproduces Newton’s gravity; in strong fields it predicts black holes, gravitational waves, and an expanding Universe.

Part I — Elements of Differential Geometry

Euclidean Geometry

In flat 3D space with Cartesian coordinates \((x,y,z)\), the metric is \(\delta_{ij}\) and the line element is \[ \mathrm d\ell^2 = \delta_{ij}\,\mathrm d x^i\,\mathrm d x^j = \mathrm d x^2 + \mathrm d y^2 + \mathrm d z^2. \] Inner products and lengths follow from this metric; rotations preserve \(\delta_{ij}\).

Natural Basis Vectors

A coordinate chart \(x^i\) induces a coordinate (natural) basis \(\{\partial/\partial x^i\}\) for tangent vectors and a dual basis \(\{\mathrm d x^i\}\) for covectors. Under a change of coordinates \(x^i\to x^{i'}\), components transform via the Jacobian: \(V^{i'} = \frac{\partial x^{i'}}{\partial x^j} V^j\). Tensors are multilinear maps built from these bases.

Metric Tensor & Line Element

The metric \(g_{\mu\nu}(x)\) generalises \(\delta_{ij}\) to curved spacetime (signature \((- + + +)\) used here). It lowers indices and defines proper time \(\mathrm d\tau\) for timelike paths via \[ -c^2\,\mathrm d\tau^2 = g_{\mu\nu}\,\mathrm d x^{\mu}\,\mathrm d x^{\nu}. \] The inverse \(g^{\mu\nu}\) raises indices and satisfies \(g^{\mu\alpha}g_{\alpha\nu}=\delta^{\mu}{}_{\nu}.\)

Affine Connection & Curvature

Metric compatibility and torsion‑free assumption give the Levi‑Civita connection (Christoffel symbols) \[ \Gamma^{\rho}{}_{\mu\nu} = \tfrac12 g^{\rho\sigma}\big(\partial_{\mu} g_{\nu\sigma}+\partial_{\nu} g_{\mu\sigma}-\partial_{\sigma} g_{\mu\nu}\big). \] Geodesics obey \[ \frac{\mathrm d^2 x^{\rho}}{\mathrm d\lambda^2} + \Gamma^{\rho}{}_{\mu\nu}\,\frac{\mathrm d x^{\mu}}{\mathrm d\lambda}\,\frac{\mathrm d x^{\nu}}{\mathrm d\lambda} = 0. \] Curvature is described by the Riemann tensor \[ R^{\rho}{}_{\sigma\mu\nu} = \partial_{\mu}\Gamma^{\rho}{}_{\nu\sigma} - \partial_{\nu}\Gamma^{\rho}{}_{\mu\sigma} + \Gamma^{\rho}{}_{\mu\lambda}\Gamma^{\lambda}{}_{\nu\sigma} - \Gamma^{\rho}{}_{\nu\lambda}\Gamma^{\lambda}{}_{\mu\sigma}. \] Contractions yield the Ricci tensor \(R_{\mu\nu}\) and scalar \(R\); the Bianchi identities imply \(\nabla_{\mu} G^{\mu\nu}=0\).

Part II — Spacetime & Causal Structure

Classical View of Newton & Galileo

Classical mechanics assumes absolute time and Galilean transformations between inertial frames. Newtonian gravity is instantaneous action at a distance with potential \(\Phi\) obeying \(\nabla^2\Phi = 4\pi G\rho\). These ideas conflict with finite signal speed and Maxwell’s equations.

Lorentz Transformations

Special Relativity replaces absolute time with invariant interval \[ \mathrm d s^2 = -c^2\,\mathrm d t^2 + \mathrm d x^2 + \mathrm d y^2 + \mathrm d z^2, \] preserved by Lorentz transformations. Consequences: time dilation \(\gamma=1/\sqrt{1-v^2/c^2}\), length contraction, and relativity of simultaneity. Four‑vectors and tensors keep equations covariant.

Relativistic Effects of Causality

Light cones partition events into timelike, lightlike, and spacelike separations. Timelike‑separated events can influence each other; spacelike ones cannot without violating causality. Proper time along timelike worldlines is invariant; null worldlines satisfy \(\mathrm d s^2=0\).

Part III — The Einstein Field Equations

The Einstein Field Equations

The geometric side is the Einstein tensor \(G_{\mu\nu}=R_{\mu\nu}-\tfrac12 R g_{\mu\nu}\). The field equations with cosmological constant are \[ G_{\mu\nu} + \Lambda g_{\mu\nu} = \frac{8\pi G}{c^4}\,T_{\mu\nu}. \] Covariant conservation \(\nabla_{\mu}T^{\mu\nu}=0\) follows from the Bianchi identities. The Newtonian limit recovers \(\nabla^2\Phi=4\pi G\rho\).

The Schwarzschild Solution

For vacuum, static, spherically symmetric spacetimes, \[ \mathrm d s^2 = -\left(1-\frac{2GM}{r c^2}\right)c^2\,\mathrm d t^2 + \left(1-\frac{2GM}{r c^2}\right)^{-1}\!\mathrm d r^2 + r^2(\mathrm d\theta^2+\sin^2\!\theta\,\mathrm d\phi^2). \] Predictions include perihelion advance, light bending, gravitational redshift, Shapiro delay, and the event horizon at \(r_s=2GM/c^2\).

Exotic Solutions: Other Black Holes, Wormholes & Cosmology

  • Kerr (rotating BH): frame dragging, ergosphere, \(a=J/Mc\), horizons \(r_\pm\).
  • Reissner–Nordström (charged BH): inner/outer horizons; extremality.
  • Wormholes: Morris–Thorne metrics require exotic matter (violations of energy conditions).
  • FLRW cosmology: homogeneous/isotropic Universe with scale factor \(a(t)\); Friedmann equations govern expansion.

Penrose Diagrams & Conformal Infinity

Conformal compactification rescales the metric to bring infinity to a finite boundary; null infinity \(\mathscr I^{\pm}\) represents endpoints of light rays. Kruskal–Szekeres coordinates extend Schwarzschild across the horizon; Penrose diagrams depict causal structure globally.

Part IV — The Physics of Black Holes

Gravitational Collapse & Particle Motion

Test‑particle motion: in Schwarzschild, timelike geodesics reduce to an effective 1D problem with potential \[ V_\text{eff}(r)=\left(1-\frac{2GM}{rc^2}\right)\!\left(c^2+\frac{L^2}{r^2}\right). \] Stable circular orbits exist for \(r>3 r_s\); the ISCO for test particles is at \(r=3 r_s\) (photons at \(r=1.5 r_s\)).

Collapse: pressureless (Oppenheimer–Snyder) collapse shows horizon formation and singularity development for sufficiently compact masses. External observers never see matter cross the horizon (in coordinate time), while infalling observers do so in finite proper time.

Further physics (Hawking radiation, BH thermodynamics, accretion disks, jets) extends beyond GR’s classical scope but fits naturally on this geometric background.
