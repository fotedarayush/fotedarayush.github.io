Estimating H₀ with Gravitational-Wave Dark Sirens

Simulations of binary black-hole (BBH) “dark sirens” + realistic galaxy catalogs to infer the Hubble constant via a joint likelihood host-association framework.

Overview

This project validates a likelihood-based scheme to statistically associate BBH events with host galaxies and combine many events to estimate the Hubble constant H₀. It blends frequentist point estimation with a Bayesian posterior across independent events.

Methods

  • Mock GW distances with 30% Gaussian uncertainty; sky localizations from a truncated von Mises–Fisher model (~5°).
  • Candidate hosts from a realistic galaxy set filtered by stellar mass.
  • Per-event joint angular × distance likelihood over galaxies; combined across N events.
  • Frequentist: maximize summed likelihood; Bayesian: multiply event likelihoods with a broad H₀ prior.

Results

  • Recovered H₀ clustered near the truth within uncertainties; Bayesian posterior consistent with simulations.
  • Spline smoothing reduced Monte-Carlo noise in likelihood curves.
