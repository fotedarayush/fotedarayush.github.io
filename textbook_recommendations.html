Textbook Recommendations

Core texts I’ve found clear and useful. Start with the “Foundation” picks, then dive deeper.

Classical Mechanics

  • Taylor — Classical Mechanics (Foundation)
  • Goldstein — Classical Mechanics (Advanced)

Electromagnetism

  • Griffiths — Introduction to Electrodynamics (Foundation)
  • Jackson — Classical Electrodynamics (Advanced)

Quantum Mechanics

  • Griffiths & Schroeter — Introduction to Quantum Mechanics (Foundation)
  • Sakurai & Napolitano — Modern Quantum Mechanics (Advanced)

Statistical Mechanics

  • Reif — Fundamentals of Statistical and Thermal Physics (Foundation)
  • Pathria & Beale — Statistical Mechanics (Advanced)

General Relativity & Cosmology

  • Ray d'Inverno — Introducing Einstein's Relativity (GR Foundation)
  • Andrew R. Liddle — An Introduction to Modern Cosmology (Cosmology Foundation)
  • John Stewart — Advanced General Relativity (Advanced)

Computation & Data

  • Press et al. — Numerical Recipes
  • Bishop — Pattern Recognition and Machine Learning

Tip: Pair each textbook with a problem source (e.g., past papers or Schaum’s outlines) and your own summary notes.
