Schwarzschild Geometry & Mercury’s Perihelion

Overview

Explores the Schwarzschild metric as a static, spherically symmetric vacuum solution, geodesic motion (free fall & orbits), horizon structure, and the relativistic perihelion advance of Mercury.

Highlights

  • Interprets coordinate vs. physical singularities and asymptotic flatness.
  • Derives perihelion precession from curvature-induced corrections to Newtonian dynamics.
  • Briefly discusses extensions to charged (Reissner–Nordström) and rotating (Kerr) spacetimes.
